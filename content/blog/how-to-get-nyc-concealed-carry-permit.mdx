Getting a concealed carry license in New York City is one of the most demanding processes in the country. It is entirely doable — but the margin for error is small, and a single missing document can set you back months. This guide walks through the whole path.

## Are you eligible?

Before anything else, three things must be true:

- You are **21 or older**.
- You are a **resident of one of the five boroughs**, or you have your **principal place of business in NYC**. (Applicants outside the city generally need a separate **Special Carry** license.)
- You are **legally allowed to possess a firearm** — no felony or other disqualifying convictions, and no disqualifying mental-health or restraining-order history.

NYC does not recognize carry licenses issued by other New York counties.

## Complete your training

New York's Concealed Carry Improvement Act (CCIA) requires **18 hours of training**: 16 hours of in-person classroom instruction plus 2 hours of live-fire range time, taught by a DCJS-approved instructor. You must also pass a written test at **80% or higher**.

## Gather your documents

This is where most applications stumble. You'll need a government photo ID, four notarized character references, a notarized cohabitant affidavit for every adult in your home, a three-year social media account list, proof of training, two photos of your gun safe, and proof of residence or business.

We cover each of these in detail in our [documents guide](/requirements).

## File and interview

The application is filed online through the NYPD Licensing portal. You'll pay roughly a **$340 license fee** plus a separate fingerprinting fee, then attend an in-person interview at the License Division, where you'll be fingerprinted and your background investigated.

## How long it takes

Plan on roughly **six months** from a complete submission to a decision. The license term is **three years**, after which you renew.
